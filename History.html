Civil Rights Expansion Timeline

1848 – Women’s Rights Movement

Key goal: Gender equality and voting rights for women

Major event: Seneca Falls Convention

Leader/Group: Elizabeth Cady Stanton & Lucretia Mott

Expanded rights: Started the organized fight that led to the 19th Amendment

1865 – 13th Amendment

Year adopted: 1865

Right expanded: Abolished slavery

Who fought for it: Abolitionists, Abraham Lincoln, Radical Republicans

Historical context: Passed right after the Civil War

Effect on democracy: Freed 4 million people and began true citizenship for Black Americans

1868 – 14th Amendment

Year adopted: 1868

Right expanded: Citizenship, due process, equal protection

Who fought for it: Radical Republicans

Historical context: Reconstruction era protection against Black Codes

Effect on democracy: Foundation for almost all later civil rights laws and Supreme Court cases

1870 – 15th Amendment

Year adopted: 1870

Right expanded: Voting rights for Black men

Who fought for it: Frederick Douglass & Radical Republicans

Historical context: Final Reconstruction Amendment

Effect on democracy: Black men voted and held office until Jim Crow laws blocked them

1920 – 19th Amendment

Year adopted: 1920

Right expanded: Women’s right to vote

Who fought for it: Susan B. Anthony, NAWSA, Alice Paul

Historical context: Women’s contributions in World War I helped win it

Effect on democracy: Doubled the electorate overnight

1950s–1960s – Civil Rights Movement

Key goal: End segregation and secure equal rights

Major event: 1963 March on Washington

Leader/Group: Martin Luther King Jr. & SCLC

Expanded rights: Civil Rights Act 1964 & Voting Rights Act 1965

1964 – 24th Amendment

Year adopted: 1964

Right expanded: Banned poll taxes in federal elections

Who fought for it: Civil rights activists & President Johnson

Historical context: Removed money barrier used to stop poor/Black voters

Effect on democracy: Huge increase in Black and poor voter turnout

1965–1970 – Latino Farmworker Movement

Key goal: Better wages and conditions for farmworkers

Major event: Delano Grape Strike & Boycott

Leader/Group: Cesar Chavez & United Farm Workers

Expanded rights: First successful farmworker unions and labor protections

1969 – LGBTQ+ Rights Movement

Key goal: End discrimination based on sexual orientation/gender identity

Major event: Stonewall Riots (1969)

Leader/Group: Marsha P. Johnson & Gay Liberation Front

Expanded rights: Started modern fight → marriage equality (2015) and anti-discrimination laws

1971 – 26th Amendment

Year adopted: 1971

Right expanded: Voting age lowered to 18

Who fought for it: Vietnam War protesters (“Old enough to fight, old enough to vote”)

Historical context: 18-year-olds were being drafted

Effect on democracy: Added millions of young voters

1990 – Disability Rights Movement

Key goal: Equal access and end disability discrimination

Major event: Capitol Crawl (1990)

Leader/Group: ADAPT & Justin Dart

Expanded rights: Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) 1990
